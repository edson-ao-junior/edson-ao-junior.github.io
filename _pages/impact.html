This page summarizes the reach and usage of this academic website, which serves as a hub for research dissemination, teaching materials, supervision records, and open science resources. The metrics presented here are intended to provide a high-level view of visibility and access, without collecting or exposing personal data.

Purpose of This Website

This website is designed to support transparency, accessibility, and reuse of academic outputs, including publications, books, research projects, supervision activities, and educational materials in Software Engineering and related areas.

It also serves as an entry point for international collaboration, student supervision, and community engagement around empirical software engineering, experimentation, open science, and digital forensics.

Website Usage Overview

Aggregated access statistics are collected using Google Analytics 4 (GA4) with IP anonymization enabled. These metrics are used exclusively to understand overall usage patterns and geographic reach of the website.

  • Page views and visits over time
  • Most accessed sections (e.g., Publications, Supervisions, Books)
  • Geographic distribution of visitors (by country)
  • Traffic sources (search engines, academic platforms, direct access)

No individual user tracking, profiling, or advertising-related data collection is performed.

Geographic Reach

Visitors to this website originate from multiple countries and regions, reflecting its role as an international dissemination platform for research and educational content.

The geographic distribution of access provides evidence of international visibility and interest in topics such as empirical software engineering, controlled experimentation, research artifacts, and open science practices.

Content Visibility

Different sections of the website receive consistent access, including:

  • Publications: journal articles, conference papers, and book chapters
  • Books: edited volumes and authored books
  • Supervisions: current and completed undergraduate and graduate supervision
  • Teaching: course-related and educational materials

This pattern indicates sustained interest from researchers, students, and practitioners.

Use in Academic Reporting

The aggregated metrics collected through this website may be used as complementary evidence in:

  • Graduate program evaluation and reporting (e.g., CAPES)
  • Research funding accountability and impact descriptions
  • Sabbatical and postdoctoral applications
  • Institutional visibility and outreach reports

These indicators are not intended to replace traditional academic impact measures (such as citations), but rather to complement them by illustrating access and dissemination.

Data Ethics and Privacy

This website follows ethical data collection practices aligned with academic and open science principles:

  • IP anonymization is enabled
  • No cookies for advertising or remarketing are used
  • No personal data is stored or processed
  • Metrics are analyzed only at an aggregated level

The sole purpose of collecting usage data is to improve content organization, accessibility, and dissemination effectiveness.
